Draw attention to specific parts of your slides using the built-in laser pointer and drawing tools.

## Overview

When presenting, you often need to highlight or point at specific content on your slides. Deckyard provides two highlighting modes:

- **Laser Pointer** - A bright dot that follows your cursor with a fading trail
- **Draw Mode** - Freehand drawing that fades away after a few seconds

Both tools work directly in the browser—no additional software needed.

![Presenter mode toolbar showing Laser, Draw, Tools, Edit, and Fullscreen buttons](/images/screenshots/presenter-mode-slide.png)

## Activating the Tools

### Toolbar Buttons

In presenter mode, the toolbar shows **Laser** and **Draw** buttons:

- Click **Laser** to toggle the laser pointer on/off
- Click **Draw** to toggle drawing mode on/off

The active button is highlighted to show which mode is enabled.

### Keyboard Shortcuts

| Shortcut | Action |
|----------|--------|
| `L` | Toggle laser pointer |
| `D` | Toggle draw mode |
| `C` | Clear all drawings |
| `P` | Toggle persistent/fading drawings |
| `Esc` | Turn off highlighter (first press) |

Press the same key again to turn the mode off, or press `Esc` to disable.

## Laser Pointer Mode

The laser pointer shows a bright red dot that follows your mouse cursor:

- Large, easy-to-see circle
- Fading trail effect for visual emphasis
- Automatically hidden when cursor leaves the slide area
- Cursor is hidden so only the laser dot is visible

**Best for:**
- Pointing at specific text or elements
- Guiding the audience's attention
- Quick highlighting during explanations

## Draw Mode

Draw mode lets you sketch directly on your slides:

- Click and drag to draw freehand lines
- By default, strokes fade out after ~3 seconds
- Crosshair cursor shows when drawing is active
- Draw multiple strokes in quick succession
- Press `C` to clear all drawings immediately

**Best for:**
- Circling important content
- Underlining text
- Drawing arrows or shapes
- Annotating diagrams

### Persistent Drawing Mode

If you want drawings to stay visible until you manually clear them:

- Press `P` during presentation to toggle between persistent and fading modes
- Or enable **"Keep drawings visible until cleared"** in Settings → Preferences → Presenter Highlighter as your default

In persistent mode:
- Drawings stay on screen indefinitely
- Press `C` to clear all drawings
- Drawings are automatically cleared when you move to the next slide
- A brief notification shows when you toggle modes with `P`

## Tips

### Combining with Fullscreen

The highlighter tools work in both windowed and fullscreen presenter mode. For the best experience:

1. Enter fullscreen mode (`F`)
2. Enable laser pointer (`L`) or draw mode (`D`)
3. Present normally with your mouse

### Turning Off

Multiple ways to disable the highlighter:

- Press the same key again (`L` or `D`)
- Click the active toolbar button
- Press `Esc` (disables highlighter, then exits fullscreen on second press)

## Customization

You can customize the highlighter in **Settings → Preferences → Presenter Highlighter**:

- **Color** - Choose any color for the laser pointer and drawing tools
- **Thickness** - Adjust the size of the laser dot and drawing stroke (1-10)
- **Persistent drawing** - Keep drawings visible until manually cleared

These settings are saved per-user and apply whenever you enter presenter mode.
