Here's an honest constraint: we don't have the time or the budget to market Deckyard as a standalone product this year. Instead of pretending otherwise, we're making that constraint part of the plan.

## The Dreamkit route

[Dreamkit](https://dreamkit.eu) is our digital sovereignty toolkit: websites, newsletters, analytics and campaign tools for organizations that want to run their digital presence on European, open source infrastructure. It has something Deckyard doesn't have yet: real organizations using it every day.

So Deckyard's first stop is inside Dreamkit. A fork of Deckyard will be bundled with the Dreamkit stack, which means existing Dreamkit clients get a presentation platform that already speaks their brand identity; the same theme definitions that drive their website drive their slides.

For those organizations, this is the turnkey promise made concrete. They never have to "adopt a new tool"; slides simply become another thing their stack does, on brand by default, hosted where the rest of their data already lives.

## What we get out of it

For Deckyard the product, this route buys the one thing early software needs most: real usage by real organizations with real deadlines. Feature requests from an actual campaign, bug reports from an actual board presentation. Every improvement flows back into the open source core that anyone can self-host.

It also keeps us honest. A product that only ever meets its makers stays theoretical. A product that has to survive a Monday morning deck for a city council does not.

## What this means for the waitlist

Deckyard as a standalone product is still coming; this website, the blog and the mailing list exist precisely so that the road there is public. If you sign up, you're not waiting in the dark: you'll see the product harden inside Dreamkit first, and you'll be the first to know when the gates open for everyone else.
